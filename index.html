Recipe for Nigerian Meat Pie

Ingredients

For the dough

  • 4 cups all purpose flour (500g)
  • 1 tsp baking powder
  • 1/2 tsp salt
  • 250g cold butter or margarine
  • 1 large egg (for dough)
  • 1/2 cup cold water
  • 1 egg yolk (for egg wash)

For the dough

  • 300g minced beef (about 3/4 lb)
  • 2 medium spoon potatoes
  • i medium carrot
  • 1 medium onion
  • 1 tsp curry powder
  • 2 tsp vegetable oil
  • 1/2 tsp thyme
  • 1 seasoning cube
  • Salt & pepper to taste
  • 2 tsp flour (to thicken filling)
  • 1/2 cup water or beef stock

Instructions

  1. Begin by cooking ground beef and aromatics like onions in a pan until the meat is no longer pink.
  2. Season the meat with ingredients like beef bouillon, curry powder, thyme, salt, and pepper
  3. Add diced vegetables such as carrots and potatoes, then simmer the mixture with water or broth until the vegetables are tender.
  4. Thicken the filling with a cornstarch or flour slurry and cook until saucy. Allow the filling to cool completely.
  5. Combine flour, baking powder, and salt in a bowl. Cut in cold butter or margarine until the mixture resembles coarse breadcrumbs. Keeping ingredients cold helps create a flaky crust.
  6. Add ice-cold water gradually and mix until a firm dough forms; avoid overmixing. Divide the dough, wrap it, and chill it for at least 30 minutes to make it easier to roll.
  7. Preheat your oven to 350°F (175°C) and prepare a baking sheet. Roll out the chilled dough and cut out circles.
  8. Place a spoonful of the cooled filling onto each dough circle, fold it over to create a semi-circle, and seal the edges.
  9. Vent the tops of the pies with a fork and brush them with egg wash for a golden finish.
  10. Bake for 25–30 minutes until golden brown. Let the pies cool slightly before serving.
